Presenting the Material

Social Function, Text Structure & Language Features

RaniI have my English speech competition tomorrow.
BudiI hope you do great! You've practiced so much.
RaniThank you. I just wish I were a little more confident.
BudiYou'll be fine. Let's hope for the best tomorrow!

Hope and wish texts are used to express, ask about, and respond to someone's hopes for the future and wishes for things that are not (yet) real. In everyday life, they also help us express gratitude and trust while still hoping for something better — for ourselves, for others, and for the world around us.

We use these expressions in many everyday situations. Here are some common contexts and example sentences:

  • 1
    Before an exam or competition
    "I hope you do well in the test tomorrow." · "I hope we win the match."
  • 2
    When someone is sick or in trouble
    "I hope you get well soon." · "I hope everything works out for you."
  • 3
    Talking about dreams for the future
    "I wish I could travel around the world." · "I wish I were a doctor already."
  • 4
    Celebrations, like New Year or a birthday
    "I wish you a wonderful year ahead." · "I hope this year brings you happiness."
  • 5
    Expressing gratitude alongside hope
    "I'm grateful for my family, and I hope we stay healthy together." This shows how hope and gratitude often go hand in hand.

    • In general, a hope/wish dialogue follows this pattern:

    • Statement of Hope/Wish — the speaker expresses a hope or wish. "I hope..." / "I wish..."
    • Elaboration — a short reason or detail behind it.
    • Response — the listener responds supportively (e.g. "Amen", "I hope so too", "Good luck").
    • Closing — closing the exchange warmly.

    Here is the structure applied to a real example — notice how each line matches one part of the pattern:

    Statement
    Sari: I hope I get accepted into the science club.

    Elaboration
    Sari: I've wanted to join since last year, but the selection is tough.

    Response
    Dika: I hope so too! You always ask such great questions in class.

    Closing
    Sari: Thanks, Dika. Fingers crossed for both of us!

    Notice that hope is for realistic outcomes, while wish is for things that are imaginary or not true right now.

    Grammar formulas

    Hope (realistic) — S + hope (that) + S + V1 / will + V1
    Example: "I hope she arrives on time." · "I hope you will succeed."

    Wish (present, unreal) — S + wish + S + were / V2
    Example: "I wish I were free today." · "I wish I had more free time."

    Wish (asking for change) — S + wish + S + would + V1
    Example: "I wish you would listen to me."

    Wish (past regret) — S + wish + S + had + V3
    Example: "I wish I had brought an umbrella."

    🌤 Hope (realistic)

    I hope you pass the exam.
    Realistic hope — simple present/future tense.
    Hopefully, it won't rain.
    An adverb meaning "I hope that...".
    Let's hope for the best.
    An invitation to stay optimistic together.
    I really hope so.
    A strong, realistic hope.
    I hope to see you soon.
    Hope + infinitive (to + verb), same subject.
    We're hoping for good news.
    Hope can also be used as "-ing", showing an ongoing hope.

    🌠 Wish (imaginary)

    I wish I were taller.
    Unreal wish — uses "were" for every subject.
    I wish I could fly.
    Impossible wish — "could" + base verb.
    If only I had more time.
    A stronger, more emotional way to say "I wish".
    I wish I had studied harder.
    Regret about the past — "had" + past participle.
    I wish you were here.
    Wishing someone were present right now — uses "were".
    I wish it would stop raining.
    Wishing a situation would change — "wish + would".

    Dika: I hope I can improve my English score. And you?

    Sari: I wish I could travel to Bali with my family.

    Dika: That sounds wonderful. I hope your wish comes true!

    Summary

    Wrapping Up & Reflecting

    Key Points

    • Hope/wish texts express hopes for the future and wishes for things not (yet) real.
    • Structure: Hope/Wish Statement → Elaboration → Response → Closing.
    • Hope expressions: I hope..., Hopefully..., Let's hope for the best, etc. (realistic, present/future tense)
    • Wish expressions: I wish I were..., I wish I could..., If only..., etc. (imaginary, past-tense form)
    • Sharing hopes and wishes respectfully also means being grateful for what we already have.
